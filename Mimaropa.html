MIMAROPA: Best time to go + How to get there

Welcome to MIMAROPA, an enchanting region in the Philippines where pristine beaches, lush forests, and vibrant culture converge to create an unforgettable travel experience. Comprising the provinces of Mindoro, Marinduque, Romblon, and Palawan, MIMAROPA offers a diverse array of natural wonders and cultural treasures waiting to be discovered.

MIMAROPA enjoys a tropical climate, making it an ideal destination year-round. However, the best time to visit varies depending on your preferences and the activities you plan to engage in.

Dry Season (November to April): This period is considered the peak tourist season, characterized by sunny days and minimal rainfall. It's perfect for beach activities, island hopping, and exploring the region's natural attractions.
Wet Season (May to October): While rainfall increases during this time, it doesn't necessarily mean constant downpours. The landscape is lush and green, and you'll find fewer crowds. This season is great for enjoying discounted rates on accommodations and experiencing MIMAROPA's cultural festivals.

By Air:
The region is served by several airports, including Puerto Princesa International Airport in Palawan, San Jose Airport in Occidental Mindoro, and Marinduque Airport in Marinduque. Major airlines operate regular flights from Manila and other key cities to these destinations.

By Sea:
If you prefer a scenic journey, ferry services connect MIMAROPA's islands. Major ports include Batangas Port for trips to Mindoro and Romblon, and Puerto Princesa Port for access to Palawan. Ferry schedules and ticket availability may vary, so it's advisable to check in advance.

By Land:
For travelers seeking an overland adventure, buses and vans ply routes to MIMAROPA from Manila and nearby provinces. The journey offers glimpses of the countryside and can be a budget-friendly option for those with time to spare.

Best place to visit

Puerto Princesa Underground River
The Puerto Princesa Underground River is one of the most fascinating natural wonders in the Philippines and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Located in the St. Paul Mountain Range on the western coast of Palawan Island, it is often hailed as one of the New Seven Wonders of Nature.

The river flows through a massive cave system adorned with spectacular stalactites and stalagmites, creating intricate formations that have been shaped over millions of years. The underground river is home to a diverse ecosystem, including various species of bats, birds, reptiles, and marine life. Some of the notable species found within the park are the Palawan Hornbill, Palawan Peacock-Pheasant, and the Palawan Bearcat. The Puerto Princesa Underground River is not only a geological marvel but also a testament to the rich biodiversity of Palawan. It offers visitors a once-in-a-lifetime experience to explore the hidden wonders of the Earth, making it a must-visit destination for nature lovers and adventurers alike.

Where to See and Do in MIMAROPA

El Nido
Discover the breathtaking landscapes of limestone cliffs, turquoise lagoons, and white sandy beaches. Enjoy island-hopping tours to spots like Big Lagoon, Small Lagoon, and Secret Lagoon.

Coron
Dive into the crystal-clear waters of Coron to explore its renowned shipwrecks, vibrant coral reefs, and stunning underwater scenery. Don't miss the iconic Kayangan Lake and Twin Lagoon.

Tubbataha Reefs Natural Park
Embark on a liveaboard diving expedition to this UNESCO World Heritage Site, renowned for its incredible marine biodiversity, including sharks, turtles, and colorful coral reefs.

Puerto Galera
Dive into one of Asia's premier dive spots, teeming with vibrant marine life and colorful coral reefs. Enjoy snorkeling, diving, and beach hopping along its picturesque coast.

Moriones Festival
Experience the vibrant Moriones Festival, celebrated during Holy Week, featuring colorful costumes, parades, and reenactments of the story of Longinus, the Roman centurion.
